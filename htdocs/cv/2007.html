Interests

After my working day is done, I can often be found relaxing in a comfortable pub chair, downing a few beers and putting the world to rights, optionally over a spot of food or a pub quiz but always with friends. If I'm at home, I'll try and catch a good comedy show on the radio, play a single video game to death or learn new ways to burn food.

I'm not a huge fan of exercise for its own sake and I don't enjoy driving all that much. As a result, I put in quite a few miles per week on foot but mostly to and from work, the supermarket or anywhere else I need to be. I enjoy train journeys and often pretend to like people who live a long way away just so I can visit them. This gives me a chance to read whatever book I've been given for Christmas or the excuse to leaf through an issue of Private Eye.

Any time which isn't filled up by the above is spent in front of a computer, working on some self-inflicted project or other. Often, these will involve getting a particularly obstinate operating system, driver or application to work as I want it to. However, the projects that engage me most are geared towards making programs which I think will produce the biggest 'wow' factor with the least amount of effort. Systems which exhibit fractal properties do this by packing infinity into small spaces but it's genetic algorithms which fascinate me the most - evolutionary systems have the best track record of making something out of nothing.
